MANGROVE FACTS AND FIGURES

Mangroves are one of the most productive, biodiverse but yet undervalued ecosystems (Thomas et al., 2017, Giri et. al., 2011). From 1980 to 2000, more than 35% of the worldâ€™s mangroves were lost Giri et. al., 2011. Drivers of these losses are either natural (changes in hydrology, sedimentation, natural hazards, etc.) or man-made (agriculture, coastal development, mangrove resource use) (Chowdhury, 2017). And any programs aimed at restoring mangroves has to specifically address the underlying (local) factors causing their loss. In this study, I will attempt to predict mangrove cover change based on adjacent land covers, hydrology, restoration efforts, and population changes over time and space, using a bayesian approach.
